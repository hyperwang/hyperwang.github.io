『Jump start Node.js』一书中的名人语录

2015-04-25

因为工作的原因需要学习一下Node.js的用法，所以找来了这本『Jump start Node.js』来读一读。薄薄150页一本的入门教材，以Node.js作为后端技术构建了一个简单了基于Web的股票交易系统原型。除了Node.js外还介绍了一些前端知识，包括Bootstrap和Backbone.js等。整体来说还算清晰完整，但最大的问题是，对于Node.js的重点和难点——异步与回调等技术，即便有所涉及，篇幅也十分有限且讲解得很浅显。

不过这本书有一个额外的小亮点：每章前都有一小段与该章内容对应的名人语录，读起来还是挺有趣味与启发的。

Chapter 1

“You see things and you say, ‘Why?’ But I dream things that never were, and I say, ‘Why not?’”
—George Bernard Shaw

Chapter 2

“I have always wished for my computer to be as easy to use as my telephone; my wish has come true because I can no longer figure out how to use my telephone.”
—Bjarne Stroustrup, creator of C++

Chapter 3

“Beware of bugs in the above code; I have only proved it correct, not tried it.”
—Donald E. Knuth

Chapter 4

“When you’re a carpenter making a beautiful chest of drawers, you’re not going to use a piece of plywood on the back, even though it faces the wall and nobody will ever see it. You’ll know it’s there, so you’re going to use a beautiful piece of wood on the back. For you to sleep well at night, the aesthetic, the quality, has to be carried all the way through.”
—Steve Jobs

Chapter 5

“If everything seems under control, you’re not going fast enough.” 
—Mario Andretti

Chapter 6

“Chaos was the law of nature; order was the dream of man.” 
—Henry B. Adams

Chapter 7

“Art is never finished, only abandoned.”
—Leonardo da Vinci
